# Wiz — fastest $100M ARR ever via hyper-velocity GTM on a burning market

## Founding insight
Assaf Rappaport, Yinon Costica, Ami Luttwak, and Roy Reznik saw that cloud security was an enterprise market on fire (post-mass-cloud-migration, every CISO needed a story for board meetings) but existing tools (CSPM products, agent-based scanners) were noisy and slow to deploy. The contrarian bet: ship *agentless* cloud security with a 5-minute deployment, run CEO-led sales, and outpace incumbents who couldn't move that fast.

## Initial wedge
Fortune 500 CISOs at AWS/GCP/Azure-heavy enterprises who were 18 months into cloud migration and getting board pressure on cloud risk. They had budget, urgency, and dissatisfaction with their current tools (Prisma, Lacework). Not SMB — explicitly enterprise from day one, because the market was on fire there.

## Timing call
2020. COVID accelerated cloud migration by 5 years; every enterprise was scrambling to secure suddenly-larger cloud footprints. Two years earlier the urgency wasn't there; two years later Palo Alto Networks would have acquired or built equivalent capabilities.

## Distribution mechanism
*CEO-led sales*. Rappaport (with previous successful exits) called the CISOs personally. 5-minute pitches, agentless deploy showed value in 30 minutes, ASCII screenshots from dashboards went viral on LinkedIn. The product was so visibly different that selling it required no marketing — just access to decision-makers, which the founder team already had from prior careers.

## 10× moment
*Time-to-first-finding*. Existing tools took weeks to deploy agents and configure. Wiz showed real cloud risks in 30 minutes via API-only deployment. The 10× was on time-to-value at the buyer's most painful moment — when they had to demo something to the board next week.

## Default-status moat
Brand monopoly on "the fastest one." By 2023 Wiz was the default cloud security RFP entry alongside Palo Alto. Google offered $23B to acquire in 2024 (Wiz declined). The combination of brand, ARR velocity, and CISO mindshare compounded faster than competitors could respond — lesson: when the market is on fire, GTM velocity (sales-led, not product-led) is the temporary moat that becomes durable mindshare.
