# OpenAI — research lab pivoted to one consumer product at the exact right moment

## Founding insight
Sam Altman and the founding team saw that an AI lab's traditional output (papers, API access for researchers) couldn't fund the compute required for AGI. The contrarian bet: package the technology into *one consumer product the world could play with in a browser*, and the product would force the funding flywheel. Not a B2B API as the wedge — a chat box on a webpage anyone could try.

## Initial wedge
ChatGPT, launched as "research preview" in November 2022. Free, no signup friction beyond email, immediately usable. The wedge wasn't an industry or job function — it was *curiosity*. Anyone could try it. The "research preview" framing also lowered expectations and PR risk in case of failure.

## Timing call
November 2022 — exactly when GPT-3.5 crossed the threshold of "useful for ordinary tasks, not just demos." Six months earlier the model wasn't there; six months later Google and Anthropic would launch competitors. The window between "good enough to amaze" and "competition arrives" was the asymmetric opportunity. They shipped in 13 days when the model was ready.

## Distribution mechanism
Pure organic virality. People screenshotting ChatGPT outputs onto Twitter, blog posts, news articles. 1M users in 5 days. 100M in 2 months — fastest consumer adoption in history. No marketing budget, no paid acquisition. The product was so far ahead of prior expectations that distribution was literally free.

## 10× moment
ChatGPT was 10× better than every prior chatbot on the SAME dimension prior chatbots had failed at: *useful conversation about anything*. Not 10× cheaper, not 10× faster — categorically more capable on the core promise. That step-change carried it past every prior failed attempt (Tay, Replika, Cleverbot).

## Default-status moat
By Q1 2023 "ChatGPT" became the verb for AI consumer use ("just ChatGPT it"). Brand monopoly + the API business that quietly built underneath on enterprise customers using GPT-4 in their own products. The consumer-product wedge created the API distribution that became the actual business — the consumer product was the marketing for the B2B layer below.
