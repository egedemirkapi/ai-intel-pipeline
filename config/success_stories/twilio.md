# Twilio — turned telecom into a programmable API

## Founding insight
Jeff Lawson saw that adding voice or SMS to an app required telecom contracts, carrier relationships, and weeks of paperwork — friction that kept communications features out of every product that needed them. The contrarian bet: same play as Stripe in payments, but for telecom — turn carrier integration into two lines of code and let developers compose communications features into anything.

## Initial wedge
Developers at startups who needed simple SMS verification, voice IVR, or 2FA — features that took 6 weeks via Verizon contracts and could be done in 6 minutes with Twilio. Indie hackers + YC startups + early SaaS companies needing notifications, exactly the segment incumbents wouldn't sell to.

## Timing call
2008-2010. Cloud + smartphones had multiplied the surface area of apps that needed phone/SMS features (Uber's driver communication, Airbnb's host messaging, every 2FA flow). Three years earlier the demand was too small to justify the API abstraction; three years later carriers would have done it themselves.

## Distribution mechanism
Pure developer word-of-mouth via Hacker News + dev conferences. Lawson personally pitched at TechCrunch Disrupt 2008. The pricing model (pay-per-message, no contracts) felt like cloud — making it irresistible to early-stage devs. Eventually grew into enterprise sales but never lost the bottom-up dev distribution.

## 10× moment
*Time-to-integrate*. SMS in your app: weeks of carrier contracts → 10 minutes of API integration. The 10× was on friction reduction, identical structurally to Stripe's payments wedge. Different domain, same lesson: developers carry their merchants with them.

## Default-status moat
Once the largest brands (Uber, Airbnb, Lyft, Netflix) had Twilio in their stacks for critical comms, switching was unthinkable — uptime guarantees, carrier relationships, deliverability data, and integration depth across thousands of apps. Twilio compounded into a $50B+ public company by acquiring adjacent comms tools (SendGrid, Segment) on top of the foundational API moat.
